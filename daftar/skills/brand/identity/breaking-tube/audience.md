## Instructions

### Primary Audience
- **Demographics**: 22-40 years old, 80% male, mix of education levels (college students to working professionals)
- **Location**: 85% India (Hindi belt + metros: Delhi NCR, UP, MP, Rajasthan, Bihar + Mumbai, Bangalore), 10% diaspora, 5% other
- **Psychographics**: Politically passionate, news-hungry, opinion-seeking, active on social media
- **Media consumption**: YouTube news, WhatsApp news groups, Twitter/X debates, TV news (transitioning to digital)
- **Pain points**: Want quick, trustworthy analysis that doesn't require a degree in political science

### Secondary Audience
- **Demographics**: 18-22, first-time voters, politically awakening
- **How they differ**: Less context, more emotionally driven, discovering news through shorts/reels
- **Content approach**: Shorter formats, more context provided, higher energy

### Tier 2/3 City Segment
- **Who**: Aspirational India — not metros, but digitally connected
- **What they want**: Hindi/Hinglish content that respects their intelligence
- **Engagement pattern**: Mobile-first, data-conscious (smaller data plans), peak evening consumption
- **Content angle**: Local/state politics connection to national stories

### Platform-Specific Audience Notes
- **YouTube**: Core audience. High engagement (likes, comments, shares). Watch time varies (5-12 min).
- **YouTube Shorts**: Massive discovery channel — younger audience finds Breaking Tube through Shorts
- **X/Twitter**: Most politically active segment. Willing to debate in comments/replies.
- **Instagram**: Younger, visual-first. Reels drive awareness, carousels for key stats.

### Content Preferences
- **Preferred formats**: Quick takes (5-8 min), YouTube Shorts, reaction-style content
- **Peak engagement topics**: BJP/Opposition dynamics, India-Pakistan, government policy impact, election analysis
- **Lower engagement**: Abstract economic theory, purely international stories
- **Engagement patterns**: Peak viewing 7-11 PM IST, spike during breaking events
