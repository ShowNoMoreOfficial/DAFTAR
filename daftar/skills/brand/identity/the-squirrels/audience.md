## Instructions

### Primary Audience
- **Demographics**: 25-45 years old, 70% male, college-educated professionals
- **Location**: 60% India (metro cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad), 25% US/UK/Canada (diaspora), 15% other
- **Psychographics**: Politically aware, globally curious, data-oriented, prefers analysis over opinion
- **Media consumption**: YouTube explainers, The Economist, Scroll.in, foreign policy podcasts
- **Pain points**: Can't find Indian-perspective analysis of global events; tired of shouting-head TV news

### Secondary Audience
- **Demographics**: 18-25, university students interested in geopolitics and current affairs
- **How they differ**: More time-rich, less domain knowledge, discovered via recommendations
- **Content approach**: Need more context/background than primary audience

### Diaspora Segment (Key Growth Segment)
- **Who**: Indian-origin professionals in US, UK, Canada, UAE, Australia
- **What they want**: Connection to India through intelligent analysis, not nostalgia
- **Engagement pattern**: Higher comment quality, more likely to share, watch at odd hours (different timezone)
- **Content angle**: "What this means for India" is always relevant to them

### Platform-Specific Audience Notes
- **YouTube**: Core audience lives here. Long watch times (avg 8+ minutes). Subscribe-and-notify behavior.
- **X/Twitter**: More politically engaged, more likely to debate. Shorter attention. Threads work.
- **Instagram**: Youngest segment. Discovery-focused. Carousels for new audience acquisition.
- **LinkedIn**: Professionals 28-50. Business/economic angles perform best.

### Content Preferences
- **Preferred formats**: 10-15 min explainers (highest), data-driven threads, infographic carousels
- **Peak engagement topics**: India-China dynamics, India-US relations, economic policy analysis, defense/security
- **Lower engagement**: Domestic electoral politics (too polarizing), cultural topics
- **Engagement patterns**: Peak viewing 6-10 PM IST, strong weekend viewership
