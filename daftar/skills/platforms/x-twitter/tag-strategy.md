## Instructions

Select hashtags and tags strategically to increase discoverability without cluttering content.

### Hashtag Rules

#### Quantity
- **Tweets**: 1-2 hashtags maximum (more than 2 reduces engagement on X)
- **Threads**: Hashtags only in Tweet 1 and last tweet
- **Zero hashtags** is acceptable for high-quality standalone takes

#### Placement
- End of tweet preferred (don't interrupt natural reading flow)
- Exception: If hashtag IS the topic, inline is fine ("The #UnionBudget2026 has three problems")
- Never start a tweet with a hashtag

#### Selection Priority
1. **Trending + relevant**: If a trending hashtag genuinely fits, use it (massive discovery boost)
2. **Topic-specific**: #IndianEconomy, #IndoPakRelations, #ModiGovernment
3. **Brand hashtag**: #TheSquirrels or #BreakingTube (use on 30-50% of tweets, not all)
4. **Event hashtags**: #Budget2026, #Elections2026 (high volume during events)

### Mention/Tag Strategy
- **Tag relevant accounts**: Government bodies, journalists, experts — if they engage, massive reach boost
- **Don't tag-spam**: Only tag accounts genuinely relevant to the content
- **Quote officials**: When referencing someone's statement, quote tweet or @ them
- **Competitor awareness**: Don't tag competitor channels (sends your audience to them)

### Trending Topic Participation
- Monitor trending topics relevant to political/economic coverage
- If a trend aligns with your expertise, tweet within 30 minutes
- Add value to the trend — don't just echo what everyone else is saying
- Use the trending hashtag but contribute original analysis

### Brand-Specific Tags

**The Squirrels**:
- #TheSquirrels, #Geopolitics, #IndiaAnalysis
- English-language tags preferred
- Professional/analytical tags over populist ones

**Breaking Tube**:
- #BreakingTube, #BhupendraChaubey, #IndianPolitics
- Mix Hindi and English tags: #ModiSarkar, #PakistanBudget
- Political/news tags match audience interest
