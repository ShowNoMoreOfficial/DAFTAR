## Instructions

Calibrate content tone for LinkedIn's professional audience and algorithm preferences. LinkedIn rewards thought leadership, not news updates.

### LinkedIn vs Other Platforms

| Dimension | YouTube/X/Instagram | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Casual to energetic | Professional, authoritative |
| Hook style | Emotional, surprising | Insight-led, career-relevant |
| Data use | Supporting argument | Leading the argument |
| Opinion | Expected, direct | Measured, well-reasoned |
| Language | Hinglish OK (Breaking Tube) | English preferred (both brands) |
| Audience expectation | Entertainment + information | Professional development + insights |

### Tone Rules for LinkedIn

#### The Squirrels on LinkedIn
- Natural fit — analytical, English, data-driven voice translates well
- Emphasize professional implications: "What this means for Indian businesses..."
- Cite more institutional sources (RBI, World Bank, IMF)
- Longer-form text posts work well — LinkedIn rewards reading time

#### Breaking Tube on LinkedIn
- Requires significant tone shift — Hinglish voice doesn't work on LinkedIn
- English-only, more restrained energy
- Focus on business/economic angles of political stories
- Position Bhupendra as veteran journalist, not populist commentator
- This is the brand's professional credibility channel

### Writing Rules
- No emojis in main text (LinkedIn audience perceives them as unprofessional)
- No ALL CAPS for emphasis — use bold or italics instead
- First-person professional voice: "In my analysis..." not "Hey guys..."
- Paragraphs of 2-3 sentences — LinkedIn rewards readability
- Ask a thoughtful question at the end to drive comments

### What Performs Well on LinkedIn
- Contrarian but substantiated takes on popular narratives
- Data visualizations with professional context
- Personal professional anecdotes tied to larger trends
- "What I learned from analyzing 100 [topic]" format
- Industry implications of political/policy changes

### What Doesn't Work on LinkedIn
- Clickbait hooks ("You won't BELIEVE...")
- Purely political opinion without business angle
- Sensationalist language
- Content that's clearly repurposed from X/Twitter without adaptation
- Short, quippy posts (work on X, flop on LinkedIn)
