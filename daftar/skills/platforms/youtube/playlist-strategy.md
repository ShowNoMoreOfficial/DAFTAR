## Instructions

Organize videos into playlists that maximize session watch time and improve content discoverability.

### Playlist Architecture

#### Playlist Types
1. **Topic Series**: Ongoing coverage of a recurring topic
   - "India-Pakistan Relations" / "India-Pakistan Analysis"
   - "Budget 2026 Coverage" / "Budget 2026 Explained"
2. **Format Series**: Same format, different topics
   - "Quick Takes" / "5-Minute Analysis"
   - "Deep Dives" / "Explainer Series"
3. **Chronological**: Time-based collections
   - "Election 2026 Coverage" — all videos from an event period
4. **Best Of**: Curated highlights
   - "Most Popular" / "Start Here" — for new subscribers

#### Playlist Naming Rules
- Include primary keyword in playlist title
- Keep under 60 characters
- The Squirrels: English, analytical framing ("India's Economic Challenges — Data Analysis")
- Breaking Tube: Hinglish, engaging framing ("Modi Sarkar ke Bade Faislay")

### Playlist Optimization
- **Description**: 100-200 words with keywords — playlists rank in YouTube search
- **Video order**: Most engaging video first (not chronological) unless it's a series
- **Thumbnail consistency**: Videos in a playlist should have visually similar thumbnails
- **Update regularly**: Add new videos to relevant playlists within 1 hour of publish

### Session Watch Time Strategy
- End each video with a verbal nudge to the playlist: "Poori series yahaan dekhiye"
- Use playlist links (not video links) in descriptions and end screens
- Create "rabbit hole" playlists: 5-8 videos that flow naturally topic-to-topic

### Playlist Maintenance
- Review monthly: remove underperforming videos, reorder by performance
- Archive seasonal playlists (election coverage) — don't delete, just deprioritize
- Maximum 20-25 active playlists — too many dilutes the channel page
