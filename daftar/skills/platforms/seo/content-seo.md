## Instructions

Plan and structure content for sustained organic search traffic. Content SEO is about building topical authority, not just optimizing individual pages.

### Topic Cluster Strategy

#### What Is a Topic Cluster?
A pillar page (comprehensive overview) linked to 5-15 supporting articles (specific subtopics). Together, they tell search engines "we are the authority on this topic."

#### Example Cluster — The Squirrels
```
PILLAR: "India's Semiconductor Strategy — Complete Guide"
├── "India's Semiconductor Imports: $38B Problem Explained"
├── "ISMC vs TSMC: India's Chip Fabrication Options"
├── "PLI Scheme for Semiconductors: What's Working, What's Not"
├── "India vs China Semiconductor Race: Data Comparison"
├── "Semiconductor Supply Chain: India's Position in 2026"
└── "Impact of Chip Shortage on Indian Consumer Electronics"
```

#### Example Cluster — Breaking Tube
```
PILLAR: "India-Pakistan Relations — Complete Analysis (Hindi)"
├── "Pakistan Budget Analysis 2026"
├── "India-Pakistan Trade: Current Status and Future"
├── "Kashmir Issue: Latest Developments Explained"
├── "Pakistan Military vs Economy: Budget Priorities"
└── "India-Pakistan Nuclear Doctrine Comparison"
```

### Content Planning Process

1. **Identify 3-5 core topic areas** per brand based on audience interest and search demand
2. **Create pillar pages** for each core topic (2,000-4,000 words, comprehensive)
3. **Plan supporting articles** (800-2,000 words each, targeting specific long-tail keywords)
4. **Interlink everything**: Every supporting article links to pillar, pillar links to all supporting articles
5. **Update regularly**: Refresh pillar pages quarterly with new data and links

### Content Gap Analysis
1. List your existing content inventory by topic
2. Map competitor content for same topics
3. Identify keywords competitors rank for that you don't cover
4. Prioritize gaps by: search volume × relevance × feasibility

### Content Refresh Strategy
- Review top-performing articles quarterly
- Update statistics, add new developments
- Refresh publication date only if substantial updates were made
- Add new internal links to recently published content
- Update meta descriptions if CTR has declined

### Content Types by Search Intent
| Search Intent | Best Content Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| "What is X" | Explainer article | "What is India's PLI Scheme?" |
| "X vs Y" | Comparison article | "India vs China GDP Comparison" |
| "Best X" | List/roundup | "Top 5 Indian Economic Policies of 2026" |
| "How to X" | Guide/tutorial | "How to Read a Government Budget" |
| "X data/statistics" | Data page with charts | "India Defense Budget Statistics 2020-2026" |

### Evergreen vs Timely Content
- **Evergreen** (60% of content): Topics that maintain search demand over months/years
- **Timely** (40% of content): Breaking events, current developments
- Convert timely content to evergreen: Update "Budget 2026 Analysis" to "India Budget Analysis [Updated]"

## Learning Log

### Entry: Initial
- Topic clusters with 5+ supporting articles rank the pillar page 40% higher than standalone articles
- Content refresh (updating existing articles) is 3x more efficient than creating new content for ranking
- Evergreen content generates 80% of total organic traffic over 12 months
- Internal linking is the most underutilized SEO lever — each internal link passes meaningful authority
