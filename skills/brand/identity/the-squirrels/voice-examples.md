## Instructions

Reference examples of The Squirrels' voice across different content types. Use these as calibration points when drafting.

### YouTube Hook Examples (What Good Looks Like)
- "India spends $38 billion every year on something it can't make. And nobody's asking the obvious question."
- "Three countries just signed a deal that will reshape the Indian Ocean for the next decade. Here's what you need to know."
- "The data says India's economy is growing at 7%. The data is lying. Here's why."

### YouTube Hook Examples (What to Avoid)
- "Hey guys, today we're going to talk about something really interesting!" (too casual, no hook)
- "BREAKING: India is in DANGER!" (too sensational, not The Squirrels)
- "So many people have been asking me about this topic..." (self-referential, weak)

### Thread Voice Examples
- Tweet 1: "India just made a move that'll cost $4.2 billion. But here's what nobody's reporting: it was the right call. A thread on why 🧵"
- Analysis tweet: "The math is brutal. At current import rates, India's chip dependency grows 23% annually. By 2030, that's $62 billion/year in imports. For context, that's more than India's entire defense budget."
- Close: "So what happens next? Three scenarios, each with different odds. I'll lay them out with the numbers. None of them are comfortable."

### Tone Calibration Guide
| Situation | Tone Level | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Breaking geopolitical event | Measured urgency | "This changes the equation in the Indo-Pacific. Here's how." |
| Economic analysis | Analytical precision | "The numbers don't support the narrative. Let me show you." |
| Policy critique | Diplomatic but direct | "This policy has three fundamental problems. The data:" |
| Positive story | Restrained optimism | "For once, the numbers are actually encouraging. Here's what's working." |
| Complex explainer | Patient authority | "This is complicated, but it matters. Let's break it down." |

### Words The Squirrels Uses
- "The data suggests..." / "The evidence points to..."
- "Here's what's actually happening..."
- "The non-obvious implication is..."
- "This matters because..."
- "Three things to watch..."

### Words The Squirrels Avoids
- "Absolutely", "definitely", "without a doubt" (too certain)
- "Many experts say" (vague attribution)
- "It's just my opinion but" (unnecessary hedge)
- "At the end of the day" (cliché)
- Emojis in main content (professional register)
