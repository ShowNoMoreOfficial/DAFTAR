## Instructions

Reference examples of Breaking Tube's voice. Hinglish is not sprinkled on top — it IS the voice.

### YouTube Hook Examples (What Good Looks Like)
- "Modi ne aaj jo kaha, uska matlab samajhiye — kyunki media galat bata rahi hai."
- "Pakistan ka ₹50,000 crore ka jhol — aur India ko iska kya fark padta hai?"
- "Yeh policy change chupke se aayi — aur iska aap par SEEDHA asar padega."

### YouTube Hook Examples (What to Avoid)
- "Hello friends, today we will discuss an important topic" (too English, no energy)
- "SHOCKING! India DESTROYED Pakistan!" (clickbait, not credible)
- "So basically, what happened is..." (slow start, weak hook)

### Hinglish Voice Rules
- **Natural code-switching**: Hindi for emotion and emphasis, English for data and analysis
- **Good**: "GDP growth 7% hai, but ground reality mein kya ho raha hai?"
- **Bad**: "GDP growth saat percent hai" (forced Hindi for numbers that are naturally English)
- **Pattern**: Statement in Hinglish → Data in English → Reaction/implication in Hindi

### Thread Voice Examples (X/Twitter)
- Tweet 1: "Pakistan ka new budget analysis — 5 points mein samjhiye kya-kya gadbad hai 🧵"
- Data tweet: "Defense budget: 40% of total spending. Education: 8%. Priorities clear hain."
- Close: "Bottom line: Pakistan ka budget survival mode mein hai. India ke liye iska matlab — detailed analysis video mein. Link 👇"

### Tone Calibration Guide
| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Breaking political news | Urgent, direct | "Abhi-abhi yeh khabar aayi hai — samjhiye kya hoga aage" |
| Policy analysis | Accessible expert | "Simple bhasha mein samjhiye — yeh policy kya karegi" |
| India-Pakistan | Passionate but factual | "Facts dekhiye, propaganda nahi — numbers yeh kehte hain" |
| Economic impact | Relatable | "Aapki jeb par kitna asar — hisaab lagaate hain" |
| Election analysis | Energetic | "Chunav ka sabse bada surprise — data analysis" |

### Words Breaking Tube Uses
- "Samjhiye" (understand) — invites the audience in
- "Seedha" / "Directly" — cuts through complexity
- "Aapki jeb par" (on your pocket) — personalizes economics
- "Data dekhiye" — establishes credibility
- "Bottom line" — audience loves clear conclusions

### Words Breaking Tube Avoids
- Excessively formal Hindi (sounds like DD News)
- Pure English without any Hindi (loses authenticity)
- Slang that alienates older audience
- Political labels (BJP-wala, Congress-supporter) — analyze parties, don't label self
