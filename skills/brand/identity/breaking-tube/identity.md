## Instructions

### Brand Name
Breaking Tube

### Client
Bhupendra Chaubey (veteran journalist)

### Channel
YouTube: Breaking Tube (100K+ subscribers)

### Tagline
Hinglish political analysis — fast, accessible, energetic

### Brand Voice
- **Tone**: Energetic, accessible, opinion-forward, Hinglish-natural
- **Register**: Conversational — like a knowledgeable friend breaking down the news over chai
- **Language**: Hinglish (natural Hindi-English code-switching, leaning Hindi for emphasis)
- **Personality traits**: Passionate, direct, populist-accessible, occasionally humorous, never boring
- **What we sound like**: A senior journalist who speaks your language, literally
- **What we don't sound like**: Academic, dry, elitist, exclusively English

### Editorial Guidelines
- **Topics we cover**: Indian politics, domestic policy, governance, elections, social issues, India-Pakistan, India-China, economic impact on common people
- **Topics we avoid**: Deep academic analysis, purely international stories without India angle, content requiring heavy English
- **Position on controversy**: Can take positions — the audience expects opinions — but must be rooted in facts, not rhetoric
- **Fact-checking standard**: Same as The Squirrels — every claim traceable to sources. Opinions clearly labeled as opinions.
- **Default diplomatic level**: Level 3 (Advocate) for domestic topics, Level 2 (Analyst) for international

### Visual Identity
- **Primary colors**: Red (#D32F2F), black, white, yellow accents
- **Typography**: Bold, impactful, readable at mobile size
- **Thumbnail style**: High-energy, bold text, expressive face shots, red/yellow accents. Dramatic but not clickbait.
- **Logo usage**: Breaking Tube logo prominent on all thumbnails

### Content Strategy
- Primary format: Quick takes (5-8 min) — speed is the competitive advantage
- Secondary: Explainers (8-12 min), YouTube Shorts
- Posting cadence: 4-5 YouTube videos/week, 5+ Shorts/week
- Target quality: Tier B-C for breaking, Tier A for weekly explainers
