## Instructions

Ensure content is culturally informed, not just factually correct. Audiences detect cultural tone-deafness immediately.

### India-Specific Cultural Intelligence

**Regional Awareness**
- India is not one audience — North/South/East/West have different media consumption patterns
- Hindi-belt content ≠ all-India content
- Always specify which India you're talking about when making generalizations

**Festival/Religious Sensitivity**
- Know the major festival calendar — avoid scheduling controversial content during festivities
- Never use religious imagery as casual illustrations
- When covering religious topics, consult sensitivity classification skill

**Political Landscape**
- India's audience is politically divided — data-first approach navigates this best
- Avoid framing that maps to specific political parties unless explicitly covering party politics
- "Development" and "nationalism" mean different things to different audiences

**Economic Class Sensitivity**
- Per-capita GDP is $2,500 — many analogies from Western media don't translate
- When discussing economic impact, always include the spectrum (urban middle class to rural poor)
- "Aspirational India" is real — don't talk down to the audience

### International Context for Indian Audience
- India's position in global events is always the entry point for Indian viewers
- "What does this mean for India?" is the implicit question in every international story
- South Asian context (Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka) is always relevant
- Indian diaspora is a significant audience segment — include their perspective

### Hinglish Guidelines (Breaking Tube)
- Natural code-switching, not forced — use Hindi phrases where they naturally fit
- Common patterns: English for data/analysis, Hindi for emotional emphasis
- Avoid pure Hindi jargon that excludes non-Hindi speakers
- Keep English subtitles for all Hindi/Hinglish content
