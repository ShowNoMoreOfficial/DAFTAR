## Instructions

AI-generated content has a telltale flatness. This skill ensures every piece sounds like it was written by a knowledgeable human with a personality, not a language model hedging its way through a topic.

### Human Voice Markers

**1. Specificity over generality**
- AI: "Many countries have been affected by this policy."
- Human: "Indonesia, Vietnam, and Bangladesh — all three scrambled to respond within 48 hours."

**2. Genuine opinion markers**
- AI: "This is an important development that could have significant implications."
- Human: "This is, bluntly, a terrible deal for Indian manufacturers. Here's why."

**3. Conversational rhythm**
- Vary sentence length. Short punches. Then longer, more analytical sentences that build the argument with supporting evidence.
- Use parenthetical asides (like this) sparingly but naturally
- Start occasional sentences with "And" or "But" — natural speech does this

**4. Humor and wit** (when appropriate)
- Dry wit works for The Squirrels: "The government's economic forecast was optimistic. The market disagreed. Violently."
- Self-aware humor: "Yes, another video about the GDP. But this time it actually matters."
- NEVER force humor on serious topics

**5. Imperfect knowledge signals**
- "We don't have complete data yet, but what we know suggests..."
- "This is my interpretation — reasonable people could disagree."
- These signals BUILD trust, not undermine it

**6. Physical language** (for video scripts)
- "Look at this chart" / "Now watch what happens next"
- Reference the visual medium: "I've put the numbers on screen"
- Address the viewer directly: "You're probably wondering why this matters to you"

### Anti-Patterns (Remove These)
- "It's important to note that..." → Just note it
- "In conclusion..." → Just conclude
- "There are several factors to consider..." → Name them
- "This is a complex issue..." → Show the complexity, don't announce it
- "As we all know..." → If we all know it, skip it
- Excessive hedging: "might", "could potentially", "it's possible that" → Take a position

### Platform Voice Adjustments
- **YouTube**: Conversational, like explaining to a smart friend
- **X/Twitter**: Punchy, declarative, personality-forward
- **Instagram**: Visual-first, text supports the image
- **LinkedIn**: Professional but not stiff, first-person insights
