## Instructions

Select optimal tags and categories to maximize YouTube search discovery and suggested video placement.

### Tag Strategy

#### Tag Hierarchy (30 tags maximum, 500 characters total)
1. **Exact match** (3-5 tags): Exact phrases viewers search for
   - e.g., "india economy 2026", "pakistan budget analysis"
2. **Broad match** (5-8 tags): Topic-level keywords
   - e.g., "indian politics", "geopolitics explained", "economic analysis"
3. **Brand tags** (2-3 tags): Channel and series identifiers
   - e.g., "the squirrels", "breaking tube", "bhupendra chaubey"
4. **Long-tail** (5-10 tags): Specific phrases with lower competition
   - e.g., "india semiconductor policy 2026 explained", "pakistan defence budget breakdown"
5. **Trending** (2-3 tags): Currently trending related terms
   - Only if genuinely relevant to content

#### Tag Research Process
1. Start with YouTube search autocomplete for primary topic
2. Check competitor videos on same topic — note their visible tags
3. Use search volume signals (autocomplete order = rough volume proxy)
4. Prioritize tags where channel has ranking potential (not dominated by massive channels)

### Brand-Specific Tag Sets

**The Squirrels — Always Include**:
- "the squirrels", "geopolitics", "india analysis"
- Topic-specific English keywords

**Breaking Tube — Always Include**:
- "breaking tube", "bhupendra chaubey", "indian politics hindi"
- Mix Hindi and English keywords: "modi news", "pakistan news hindi"

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't use irrelevant trending tags (YouTube penalizes this)
- Don't repeat the same keyword with minor variations
- Don't use single-word tags (too competitive, too vague)
- Don't copy entire competitor tag sets — tailor to your content
