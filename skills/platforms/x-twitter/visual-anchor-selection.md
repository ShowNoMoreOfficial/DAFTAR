## Instructions

Select and optimize visuals that stop the scroll and anchor tweet content in the X/Twitter feed.

### Visual Types by Effectiveness

#### 1. Custom Charts/Infographics (Highest Engagement)
- Data visualizations created for the specific tweet
- Clean, branded, readable on mobile
- Single insight per chart — don't cram
- Use brand colors (Squirrels: navy/teal, Breaking Tube: red/black)

#### 2. Screenshots with Annotation
- Screenshots of source documents, news headlines, data tables
- Add circles, arrows, highlights to draw attention to key info
- Red circles/arrows for Breaking Tube, teal for Squirrels

#### 3. Quote Cards
- Key quote on branded background
- Large text, readable without expanding
- Source attribution visible

#### 4. Thumbnail-Style Graphics
- YouTube thumbnail repurposed for X
- Works well for video promotion tweets
- Ensure text is readable at X's smaller image size

#### 5. Maps and Geo Visuals
- For geopolitical content — highly shareable
- Annotated maps with data overlays
- India-focused audience loves comparative maps

### Image Specifications
- **Aspect ratio**: 16:9 (1200x675px) for single image
- **Multi-image**: 2 images side by side, or 4-grid for data series
- **File size**: Under 5MB, prefer PNG for charts/text, JPEG for photos
- **Text on images**: Minimum 24pt font — must be readable on mobile without expanding

### Alt Text Requirements
- Always include descriptive alt text (accessibility + SEO)
- Describe what the visual shows, including key data points
- Example: "Bar chart showing Pakistan's budget allocation: Defense 40%, Education 8%, Health 6%, Infrastructure 12%"

### What NOT to Use
- Stock photos (looks generic, kills credibility)
- Low-resolution images (pixelated = amateur)
- Images with too much text (becomes unreadable)
- Memes (not appropriate for analytical brands)
- Watermarked content (legal and credibility issues)

### Entry: Initial
- Tweets with custom charts get 2.3x more engagement than text-only
- Single-insight charts outperform multi-data charts (simplicity wins on X)
- Screenshots with annotation feel more "authentic" than polished graphics for breaking news
