## Instructions

Manage replies and community engagement to build audience loyalty and increase algorithmic reach.

### Reply Priority Framework

#### Tier 1 — Respond Within 15 Minutes
- Verified accounts / journalists engaging with your content
- High-follower accounts (10K+) replying or quoting your tweets
- Factual corrections that need addressing
- Questions from loyal/repeat engagers

#### Tier 2 — Respond Within 1 Hour
- Substantive questions or counter-arguments
- Replies with significant engagement (10+ likes on the reply)
- Mentions in relevant threads by others

#### Tier 3 — Respond When Possible
- General positive comments (a like is sufficient)
- Simple questions that are answered in the linked video
- Repeat questions (consider pinning an FAQ-style reply)

#### Ignore / Don't Engage
- Trolls seeking attention (engagement rewards them)
- Bad-faith arguments (no amount of data will change their mind)
- Spam or promotional replies
- Personal attacks — block and move on

### Reply Tone by Brand

**The Squirrels**:
- Professional but approachable
- Reply with additional data when challenged: "Good question — the data actually shows..."
- Never get into flame wars — maintain analytical credibility
- Use replies to add context that didn't fit in the original tweet

**Breaking Tube**:
- Conversational Hinglish
- More personality — can be witty, direct
- Engage passionately but don't lose factual grounding
- Reply to audience in their language (Hindi replies to Hindi comments)

### Engagement Tactics
- **Pin best reply**: If you write a great reply to your own tweet, pin it
- **Quote-tweet interesting replies**: Amplifies discussion and rewards the replier
- **Create reply threads**: If a reply needs depth, thread your response
- **Like liberally**: Like positive replies, thoughtful critiques, good questions — it's free engagement

### Community Building
- Recognize repeat engagers by name/handle over time
- Create inside jokes/references that loyal followers appreciate
- When audience provides useful information/corrections, credit them publicly
- Host occasional open-ended questions: "What should we cover next?"
