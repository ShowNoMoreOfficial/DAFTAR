## Instructions

Prepare everything needed for a successful interview or podcast recording.

### Pre-Interview Research
1. Build a mini fact dossier on the guest: background, recent statements, expertise areas
2. Identify 3-5 topics the guest can uniquely contribute to
3. Find potential controversies or challenging points to address (diplomatically)
4. Review audience interest signals — what would our viewers want to ask this person?

### Question Architecture

**Opening (warm-up)**: 1-2 questions the guest will enjoy answering
**Core (substance)**: 5-8 questions on the main topic, ordered from broad to specific
**Challenge (tension)**: 1-2 questions that push back on the guest's known positions
**Close (memorable)**: 1 surprising or personal question + forward-looking question

### Question Types
- **Open-ended**: "Walk us through your thinking on..."
- **Data-prompted**: "The data shows X — how do you reconcile that with your position?"
- **Audience proxy**: "Our viewers are asking — why should they care about..."
- **Follow-up triggers**: Mark questions where you expect the answer to lead somewhere interesting

### Production Notes
- Record at least 20% more than planned duration — gives editing flexibility
- Capture B-roll of guest if in-person (arrival, setup, candid moments)
- Test audio levels before starting — guest mic quality is non-negotiable
- Record a separate intro/outro after the interview for clean editing

### Post-Production Opportunities
- Full interview → YouTube long-form
- Best 3-5 minutes → YouTube Short
- Key quotes → X thread with video clips
- Insights → Instagram carousel
- Professional takeaways → LinkedIn post
